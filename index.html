Following Ear

A listening hour

St. Sigfrids folkhögskola (Kronoberg 1, 352 63, Växjö) on the 30th of May at 17:00

A short practice of listening across the grounds.

We begin from a simple thought: we are never only listening to a place. The place listens back. We are inside the sound, not outside it.

The forest is not a background we enter. The building is not separate from the bodies inside it. The lake is not the thing we observe from the shore. The wind moves through us as much as it moves through the trees. The air in our lungs was just inside another body. There is no clean line between us and what surrounds us. To listen is not to receive something at a distance. It is to be inside what is already passing through.

Following Ear works from this. The practice is shaped by small invitations to the body. The body is asked to do specific things. What changes is not the landscape but the listener. The work happens inside.

We are interested in listening as a way of undoing the idea that the place around us is silent, passive, or outside us. Sounds are not only things to record. They are ways the place acts on us. A branch, water, wind, a bird, a distant machine, and footsteps on moss, all of these are part of how the place becomes present.

We do not introduce new sounds. We do not bring contrast from outside. The contrast happens internally, between how we normally are and how we suddenly are when we slow down enough that the place can reach us. The oro of the day arrives through this, not as something added, but as something the body discovers in attending differently. The hour holds scattered practice. Different bodies, different rhythms, different distances. What connects it is not that everyone walks the same path but that we are all trying to listen from within the same field of relations.

We meet at a certain point, at the end of the hour, to reflect together.

Sound Ribbons

For this project, the participants will get colored ribbons which they can place along forest paths and in trees to indicate the origin of different natural sound sources. Each color represents a specific emotional tone, helping participants navigate the environment in a more mindful and sensory way.

Rather than just guiding direction, the ribbons create a quiet emotional map of the forest experience, encouraging awareness, reflection and presence through color and sound.


Color guide:

  • Blue: calm, introspection, depth
  • Lavender: reflection, imagination, gentle awareness
  • Red: grounding, presence, disturbing
  • Turquoise: clarity, openness, breath
  • Peach: gentleness, connection, warmth

Sound Mapping

As participants move through the environment while listening to the sound-based journey, their routes, recorded by Strava, gradually form a layered map of how people are physically guided, paused, or drawn through space.

Each path becomes a trace of perception: where people slowed down, where they lingered and which sound moments influenced direction. These overlapping routes reveal patterns of attention and emotional response, showing how they experienced the forest.


How to use Strava for this project

1. Start Strava before beginning the forest walk and enable GPS tracking.
2. Follow the sound experience naturally, without focusing on performance or distance.
3. Let the audio guide your movement; pausing, slowing, or changing direction when you feel drawn to a sound.
4. After finishing, save the activity as a walk or hike.
5. Share your journey!

The forest becomes a shared composition built from many individual walks. Please share your map at sc223bx@student.lnu.se, so we can create a collage.
